Investigating the Umpire’s Decision-making in Leg Before Wicket (LBW) Situations: A Bayesian Brain Approach

Introduction: It’s the season of cricket fever, with IPL, T20s, and test matches! Yet behind the scenes, umpire decisions in LBW situations often draw controversy...

Research Hypothesis: The study explores how umpires integrate visual cues, psychological elements, and environmental noise into their decision-making using the Bayesian Brain Model...

Methodology: Using MATLAB simulations, multiple balls were modeled in various scenarios. The trajectory, umpire view, prior biases, and decision rules were plotted and analyzed...

Factors Influencing Decisions: Crowd noise, sound differentiation, wicketkeeper movement, player importance, umpire confidence, and more all played roles in the final decisions made by the AI model...

Results: The Bayesian model achieved ~91.6% decision accuracy. Simulated umpires showed confidence shifts based on previous correctness and external cues...

Conclusion: This study mimics human-like umpire behavior, demonstrating how subjective and objective factors influence decision-making in cricket. The approach paves the way for further exploration in cognitive modeling in sports.
