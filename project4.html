üß† Effects of Spaceflight on Astronaut Brain Structure

Roberts DR et al., NEJM, 2017

Methods:

  • Compared pre- and post-flight MRIs of 34 astronauts.
  • 18 astronauts had long-duration ISS missions (~165 days).
  • 16 had short-duration Shuttle missions (~13 days).
  • MRI cine clips were used to analyze cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) spaces and brain displacement.

Key Results:

  • 94% of long-duration astronauts had narrowing of the central sulcus, vs. 19% of short-duration astronauts.
  • Upward brain shift and CSF space compression at the vertex observed in all long-duration missions.
  • Ventricular volume increased by 11% on average after long-duration spaceflight.
  • Three astronauts with long missions showed optic disc edema, linked to elevated intracranial pressure (VIIP syndrome).

This study provides compelling evidence that prolonged microgravity can induce structural brain changes and fluid shifts in astronauts.
